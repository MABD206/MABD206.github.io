About

Turning data into conservation impact

I'm a Data Analyst at DTU Aqua (Technical University of Denmark) where I lead and contribute to research initiatives spanning fisheries management, marine species conservation, and advanced data analytics.

My work bridges deep learning, computer vision, and statistical analysis — from training YOLO-based object detection models for bycatch identification to developing CNNs for fish age estimation from otolith images.

I hold a Master's degree in Data Science & AI from Chalmers University, where my thesis pioneered federated learning for ETP bycatch detection using electronic monitoring video feeds.

I presented two posters at the ICES Annual Science Conference 2024 in Gateshead, UK, and regularly contribute processed datasets to ICES for international fisheries stock assessments.
